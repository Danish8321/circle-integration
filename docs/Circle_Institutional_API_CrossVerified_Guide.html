Reconciling circle-institutional-api-guide.html (existing project doc, External Entities model) against fresh research on developers.circle.com, including the official OpenAPI reference schemas, so both documents agree and every claim is traceable to an official source.

Cross-Verification Summary

✅
Your existing guide's core model checks out

Every endpoint your circle-institutional-api-guide.html uses — /v1/externalEntities, /v1/businessAccount/banks/wires, /v1/businessAccount/wallets/addresses/deposit, /v1/businessAccount/wallets/addresses/recipient, /v1/businessAccount/transfers, /v1/businessAccount/payouts, /v1/notifications/subscriptions — is confirmed live in Circle's current official API reference, under a category Circle itself labels "Core API for Institutions → External Entities." This is not a deprecated or legacy path; it is a real, currently-documented product, gated the same way as everything else institutional ("contact your Circle account representative").

⚠️
One real correction found: there is no same-account wallet-to-wallet transfer endpoint

My earlier draft (and an implicit assumption in your architecture) treated "sweep client wallet balance to the Main/Distributor wallet" as a simple internal transfer call. The official OpenAPI schema for POST /v1/businessAccount/transfers only accepts destination.type of verified_blockchain or cross_entity_master_wallet (a sibling Circle Mint Account, i.e. a different legal entity's account — not a plain wallet inside your own account). There is no documented request shape that moves value directly from an External Entity wallet to your own Distributor/master wallet as an off-chain ledger entry. See Section 3 for what to do instead.

ℹ️
A second, newer Circle product also matches your requirement

Circle now also offers Digital Asset Accounts (DAA) — a distributor product with a formal Program → Account → Sub-account hierarchy and instant, fee-free internal transfers between sub-accounts (this is the capability your architecture actually wants for the sweep step). It is a separate, newer, equally-permissioned product from External Entities, not a replacement for it. See Section 1 for the comparison and why staying on External Entities is still the pragmatic choice given your existing build.

3. Correction: The Client-Wallet-to-Main-Wallet Sweep

This is the one place fresh verification changes the design, so it gets its own section.

What was assumed

That moving settled stablecoin value from a client's External Entity wallet into your own Distributor/master wallet could be done with a simple internal transfer call — mirroring how Digital Asset Accounts' account-to-account transfer works.

What the official schema actually allows

The OpenAPI spec for POST /v1/businessAccount/transfers restricts destination to exactly two shapes:

destination: oneOf
  - { type: "verified_blockchain", addressId: "<pre-approved recipient address>" }
  - { type: "cross_entity_master_wallet", entityId: "<sibling Circle Mint Account's entity id>" }

Neither shape is "move to a wallet inside my own account." cross_entity_master_wallet is for transferring between two separate, sibling Circle Mint Accounts (e.g. if your organization holds more than one CMA) — it does not apply to moving value from one of your own External Entities to your own primary/master wallet.

What is actually documented and available

OptionMechanismTrade-off
A — Redeem to your own bank accountPOST /v1/businessAccount/payouts with source.id = the client's walletId, destination = your own linked wire/SEPA bank account (per your guide's existing pattern).Exits Circle as fiat — this is a genuine redemption, not a stablecoin-to-stablecoin move. Matches the literal words "deduct... into our account" if your Main position is meant to be fiat, not a Circle wallet balance. Fee is deducted (Section 2).
B — On-chain transfer to your own addressRegister your own Main wallet's receiving address as a recipient (POST /v1/businessAccount/wallets/addresses/recipient), get it approved in Mint Console, then POST /v1/businessAccount/transfers with destination.type: verified_blockchain pointing at that address.Stays in stablecoin form, but is a real blockchain transaction: network fee, confirmation delay, and (above the Travel Rule threshold) originator identity requirements, exactly as for any external payout.

Worth a direct question given how central this step is to your flow.
⚠️
Action item

Decide, with your Circle account team, whether Option A (redeem-to-fiat, which is what your existing guide's payout step already documents) is in fact your intended "Main wallet" position — or whether you genuinely need to hold swept value as stablecoin at the Distributor level, in which case Option B (on-chain, fee-bearing) is the only documented path today, and Digital Asset Accounts (Section 1) is the only Circle product with a free instant sweep.

Persist walletId as the entity-scoped primary key in your database against your internal ClientCompanyId. Every subsequent call — wires, deposit addresses, transfers, payouts, balances — is scoped by this walletId.

5. FIAT Transactions — USD & EUR

5.1 Linking & wire instructions

POST /v1/businessAccount/banks/wires
{ idempotencyKey, accountNumber, routingNumber, billingDetails{...}, bankAddress{...} }
→ { id, trackingRef, status }

GET /v1/businessAccount/banks/wires/{id}/instructions?walletId={entity walletId}
→ { trackingRef, beneficiary{...}, beneficiaryBank{ name, routingNumber, accountNumber, currency, country } }

The trackingRef returned when walletId is supplied is scoped to that entity — a wire quoting it is routed by Circle directly to the entity's wallet, not your Distributor's primary wallet.

5.2 Deposit (funding)

Client's bank sends USD wire or EUR wire/SEPA quoting the trackingRef. Circle settles and auto-mints USDC (USD) or EURC (EUR) into the entity wallet. A deposits webhook fires with destination.id = walletId and status: complete.

5.3 Withdrawal / redemption

POST /v1/businessAccount/payouts
{ idempotencyKey, source:{type:"wallet", id: walletId}, destination:{type:"wire"|"sepa"|"sepa_instant", id: bankAccountId}, amount:{amount, currency} }
→ { id, sourceWalletId, destination, amount, toAmount, fees, status, trackingRef, riskEvaluation, return }

toAmount is the net amount actually wired after Circle's fee; a later bank-side return populates the return object and re-credits the source wallet — monitor the payouts webhook for this after the initial complete status, not just once.

6. On-Chain Transactions — USDC & EURC

CurrencyAPI codeChains (subset)
USDCUSDEthereum, Base, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Polygon PoS, Solana, Stellar, Algorand, Aptos, Sui, XRPL, Noble, Hedera, OP Mainnet, World Chain, and more
EURCEUREthereum, Base, Avalanche, Solana, Stellar — a materially narrower set than USDC

Receiving: POST /v1/businessAccount/wallets/addresses/deposit (body: walletId, currency, chain) — one address per (entity, chain); reuse it for every future deposit. Verify settlement via GET /v1/businessAccount/transfers?walletId=…, which reaches complete after the chain's required confirmation count.

Sending: two-stage — POST /v1/businessAccount/wallets/addresses/recipient registers the destination (pending Mint Console admin approval), then POST /v1/businessAccount/transfers with source.id = walletId and destination:{type:"verified_blockchain", addressId}. Always set source explicitly — omitting it defaults to your master/main wallet, not the entity's.

Travel Rule: transfers ≥ USD 3,000 on in-scope chains require originator identity. Business-account transfers use your on-file company identity automatically; third-party crypto payouts via POST /v1/payouts require explicit source.identities[].

8. Webhooks

v1 (Amazon SNS) scheme — subscribe via POST /v1/notifications/subscriptions, complete the SNS SubscriptionConfirmation handshake, then verify every delivery by SNS signature (not IP allow-listing, since v1 traffic originates from AWS SNS IP ranges). Topics relevant here: externalEntities (compliance decisions), deposits (fiat + on-chain funding), transfers (outbound/on-chain status), payouts (redemption + returns), addressBookRecipients (recipient approval). Sandbox allows up to 3 active subscriptions; production allows 1. The customAttributes field is output-only — Circle sets {"clientId": "…"} on every notification; it is never a request parameter.

9. Business Scenarios

A. New client onboarding

Create the external entity, wait for (or poll for) ACCEPTED, persist walletId against the client's internal record. A REJECTED entity is permanent; the remedy is a fresh POST /v1/externalEntities with corrected details.

B. USD wire funding

Link bank → get entity-scoped instructions and trackingRef → client wires → Circle mints USDC → deposits webhook → verify → credit FundAccount → move value to Main (Section 3).

C. EUR / SEPA funding

Identical mechanics using the EUR currency code; Circle routes over SEPA or another regional rail where supported; credited stablecoin is EURC. Track USD and EUR as separate ledgers per client, matching Circle's per-currency wallet model.

D. On-chain USDC deposit

Share the entity's chain-specific deposit address; client sends USDC on that exact chain; settles after required confirmations; same verify → credit → move-to-Main sequence. Warn clients explicitly: wrong-chain or unsupported-token sends are unrecoverable.

E. On-chain EURC deposit

Same as D, constrained to EURC's five supported chains. Outside that list, redirect the client to a EUR wire deposit instead.

F. Client withdraws on-chain

Register + get Mint-Console approval for the destination address, then transfer with explicit source.id = walletId. Apply Travel Rule identity fields for third-party payouts ≥ USD 3,000.

G. Client withdraws to fiat

Check available balance, then POST /v1/businessAccount/payouts. Continue monitoring after complete — bank-side returns re-credit the wallet via the return object and must be re-reconciled against the FundAccount.

H. Settlement / Main-position accounting

Per the corrected mechanism (Section 3), every verified inbound settlement triggers either a redemption-to-own-bank or an on-chain sweep to a company-controlled address — never an assumed free internal transfer.

I. Missed webhook

Reconciliation job lists recent deposits per walletId, finds a settled item with no FundAccount credit, self-heals and flags for audit.

J. KYB rejection

complianceState: REJECTED is permanent for that entity; submit a new POST /v1/externalEntities with corrected data under the same client relationship.
